Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

1875-1950

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31 October 1875 in Nadiad, Gujarat, India. He belonged to a simple farmer family. From childhood, he was disciplined, courageous, and hardworking. He studied law and became a successful lawyer, known for his honesty and strong personality.

Role in Indian Independence :-

Sardar Patel was a senior leader of the Indian National Congress. He worked tirelessly for national unity and discipline. He was known as a strict but fair leader who believed in action more than words. Integration of Princely States Afterindependence in 1947, India had more than 560 princely states. As India’s first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister, Patel successfully united these states into one nation using diplomacy, persuasion, and firmness. Because of this great achievement, he is called the “Iron Man of India.”

Death Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel passed away on 15 December 1950. His death was a great loss to the nation, but his legacy continues to inspire millions. Statue of Unity To honor him, the Statue of Unity was built in Gujarat. It is the world’s tallest statue (182 meters) and symbolizes national unity and strength.
